    Who is Liz Cheney?

    Elizabeth Lynne Cheney is an American attorney and politician. She represented Wyoming’s at-large congressional district in the U.S. House of Representatives from 2017 to 2023 and served as chair of the House Republican Conference—the third-highest position in the House Republican leadership—from 2019 to 2021.

    Liz Cheney biography

    During the George W. Bush administration, Liz Cheney held various positions within the U.S. State Department, including Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for Near Eastern Affairs and Coordinator for Broader Middle East and North Africa Initiatives.

    She played a significant role in advocating for regime change in Iran while leading the Iran Syria Policy and Operations Group alongside Elliott Abrams. In 2009, Cheney co-founded Keep America Safe, a nonprofit organization focused on national security issues that supported the positions of the Bush-Cheney administration.

    Although she initially ran for the U.S. Senate in Wyoming in 2014, challenging incumbent Mike Enzi, she later withdrew from the race. In the House of Representatives, she occupied the same seat previously held by her father from 1979 to 1989.

    Recognized as a prominent conservative figure in the Bush-Cheney tradition and a representative of the Republican establishment, Cheney is known for her pro-business stance, emphasis on national security, unwavering support for the U.S. military, and assertive foreign policy views. She was once regarded as a leader within the neoconservative wing of the Republican Party and consistently supported the overall agenda of former President Donald Trump, despite occasionally criticizing his administration’s foreign policy.

    Cheney notably backed the second impeachment of Donald Trump due to his involvement in the storming of the U.S. Capitol in 2021. Her stance on the Capitol riot, impeachment vote, and opposition to Trump’s false claims of a stolen election led to multiple attempts by pro-Trump members of the House Republican Conference, particularly those from the Freedom Caucus, to remove her from party leadership.

    Despite House Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy supporting her removal, Cheney was ultimately ousted from her position in May 2021. In July 2021, Speaker Nancy Pelosi appointed Cheney to the House Select Committee on the January 6 Attack, and she later became the vice chair of the committee. As a result of her service on the Select Committee, Cheney’s role has been significantly impacted.
